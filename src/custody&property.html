Frequenty asked questions

What is domestic violence?

Domestic violence, also known as intimate partner violence (IPV), involves a pattern of abusive behavior in a relationship where one partner seeks to gain power and control over the other through physical, emotional, psychological, or sexual means.

What are common forms of domestic violence against women?

Common forms include physical violence (such as hitting, slapping, or choking), emotional abuse (including threats, intimidation, and isolation), sexual abuse, financial control, and stalking.

How prevalent is domestic violence against women?

Domestic violence affects millions of women worldwide. Statistics show that a significant percentage of women experience some form of domestic abuse in their lifetime.

What are the warning signs of domestic violence?

Warning signs may include frequent injuries, unexplained bruises or marks, social isolation, changes in behavior, fear of a partner, controlling behavior from a partner, or feelings of low self-esteem.

What should I do if I am experiencing domestic violence?

f you are in immediate danger, call emergency services (100 or local emergency number). Seek help from a trusted friend, family member, or domestic violence hotline. Develop a safety plan and consider contacting local shelters or organizations for support.

How can I help a friend or loved one experiencing domestic violence?

Listen non-judgmentally, offer support and resources, encourage them to seek help, and help them develop a safety plan. Avoid pressuring them to leave the relationship but express concern for their safety.
